Undergraduate Research: Understanding

Provost's Undergraduate Research Scholarship: Spring 2026

Last semester, I received the Provost's Undergraduate Research Scholarship (PURS). Now, under Dr. Therese Wu at UH, I am pursuing research in differential geometry.

My research sits at the intersection of geometry, computer science, and artificial intelligence. At a high level, I study how the shape of an object encodes important information—and how modern AI models learn and use that information.

My task is to determine the reliability of a network developed by Dr. Wu and her peers. Like many modern AI systems, this model behaves like a “black box.” It produces accurate answers, but it’s not clear what geometric information it is actually paying attention to. If the model is relying on accidental patterns in the data rather than real shape features, it could fail in new or important situations.
